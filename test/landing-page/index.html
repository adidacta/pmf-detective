Questions & Answers

What exactly is YouNote?

YouNote is a web app where you paste a YouTube URL and watch the video in a focused, distraction-free player. As you watch, press a key to add timestamped notes. Later, search across all your notes and click any timestamp to jump back to that exact moment.

How is this different from NoteGPT, Glasp, or similar tools?

Most tools focus on AI summaries or automatic transcription. YouNote is for people who want to take their own notes - because you remember what you write, not what AI writes for you. The focus is on fast note-taking with timestamps and powerful search, not AI-generated content.

Where are my notes stored?

Notes are stored in the cloud so you can access them from any device. You can export everything to markdown anytime - your data is never locked in. We're also exploring local-first storage options for those who prefer it.

Does it work with Obsidian or Notion?

Yes! Export your notes as markdown files and import them into Obsidian, Notion, or any tool that supports markdown. Timestamps are preserved as clickable links that open the video at that moment.

Does it work with Udemy, Coursera, or other platforms?

Currently YouNote works with YouTube only. Support for other platforms like Udemy, Coursera, and Vimeo is on the roadmap based on user demand.

What happens if YouNote shuts down?

Your notes export to standard markdown files - you always have a local copy. Even if YouNote disappeared tomorrow, all your notes and timestamps remain yours in a format that works anywhere.

What's the pricing?

Early access users get YouNote free while we're in beta. We'll introduce pricing later, likely a simple subscription around $8-12/month. Early adopters will get a significant discount or grandfathered free access - details TBD.

How long until I get access?

We're onboarding early access users in batches. Most people get access within 1-2 weeks of signing up. You'll get an email when your spot is ready.
